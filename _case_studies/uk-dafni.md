# Sections
sections:
  - id: overview
    icon_emoji: "&#x1F4BB;"
    content: |
      **DAFNI (Data &amp; Analytics Facility for National Infrastructure)** is a UK national research platform funded by UKRI and led by the Science and Technology Facilities Council (STFC). It provides researchers and policy-makers with high-performance computing resources, shared datasets, and a growing library of simulation models to explore how national infrastructure systems interact and respond to future pressures.

      DAFNI enables system-of-systems analysis spanning energy, transport, water, digital communications, and other critical sectors. By providing a shared computational environment, it reduces duplication of effort and facilitates collaboration across disciplines and institutions.

  - id: timeline
    content: |
      **2017:** DAFNI concept funded under UKRI Strategic Priorities Fund. **2019:** Platform launched and operational. **2020&ndash;present:** Ongoing expansion of model library, datasets, and user community. Location: **United Kingdom**.

  - id: stakeholders
    content: |
      **STFC** (Science and Technology Facilities Council) as lead operator. **UKRI** (UK Research and Innovation) as funder. University research groups across engineering, geography, and environmental science. Government departments including DEFRA, DfT, and BEIS. Infrastructure operators and consultancies.

  - id: digitalisation
    content: |
      DAFNI provides a cloud-hosted, HPC-enabled platform for running infrastructure models at scale. Key digital capabilities include: containerised model execution, large-scale dataset hosting and sharing, workflow orchestration for multi-model pipelines, and visualisation tools for spatial and temporal outputs.

      The platform supports open and reproducible modelling practices, enabling researchers to share models and datasets within a governed environment.

  - id: hazards
    type: hazards
    exogenous: "Climate change impacts including flooding, heatwaves, and sea-level rise; demographic shifts and population growth placing stress on infrastructure capacity."
    endogenous: "Data quality and availability challenges across sectors; model maturity variations; capability gaps in cross-sector integration and validation."

  - id: cost-benefit

      **Benefit:** Reduces duplication of modelling effort across research groups; enables scenario-based policy analysis at national scale; supports evidence-based infrastructure investment decisions; accelerates cross-sector collaboration.
