# Sections
sections:
  - id: overview
    icon: bike
    content: |
      Shahe Metro Station in Beijing serves as a critical transit node, handling approximately **100,000 daily trips** and generating intense demand for non-motorised vehicle (bicycle and e-bike) parking. Chronic congestion around the station &mdash; with bikes overflowing into pedestrian walkways and road margins &mdash; created safety hazards, impeded access, and overwhelmed manual management efforts.

      The response combined **physical infrastructure redesign** (dedicated parking zones totalling 8,000 spaces) with a **digital governance layer** comprising AI-powered video analytics, a user-facing mobile application, a behavioural credit system, and real-time operational dashboards. This integrated approach treats chronic urban congestion not merely as a traffic problem but as a **resilience challenge** requiring coordinated digital and physical interventions.

  - id: timeline
    content: |
      **Pre-intervention:** Chronic parking congestion around Shahe Metro Station overwhelms manual management. **Phase 1:** Physical redesign creating 8,000 dedicated parking spaces across designated zones. **Phase 2:** Deployment of AI video analytics, mobile app, and credit system. **Ongoing:** Continuous monitoring and adaptive management. Location: **Beijing, China**.

  - id: stakeholders
    content: |
      **Beijing district authorities** (infrastructure owner and lead agency). **Municipal transport commission** (policy coordination and oversight). **Technology providers** (AI video analytics and app development). **Station operators** (day-to-day management). **Commuters** (end users, participants in credit system).

  - id: digitalisation
    content: |
      **Mobile Application:** User-facing app for locating available parking spaces, navigating to designated zones, and managing personal parking accounts.

      **AI Video Analytics:** Computer vision systems monitoring parking occupancy, detecting violations (e.g. parking outside designated areas), and tracking usage patterns in real time.

      **Credit System:** Behavioural incentive framework rewarding compliant parking behaviour and penalising violations, creating a feedback loop that shapes user habits over time.

      **Operational Dashboards:** Real-time management interfaces showing occupancy rates, congestion hotspots, violation alerts, and trend analytics for operational decision-making.

  - id: hazards
    type: hazards
    exogenous: "Peak demand surges during rush hours and special events, seasonal variations in cycling volumes, sudden policy changes affecting commuter behaviour."
    endogenous: "Chronic congestion exceeding designed capacity, governance overload from manual enforcement limitations, user non-compliance with parking regulations, technology dependence risks."

  - id: cost-benefit
    content: |
      **Cost:** Capital investment in physical parking infrastructure (8,000 spaces), AI video analytics hardware and software, mobile application development and maintenance, and ongoing operational staffing.

      **Benefit:** Reduced pedestrian safety hazards from overflow parking, improved station access and throughput, lower manual enforcement costs through automated monitoring, behavioural change through credit system incentives, and real-time data enabling proactive rather than reactive management.
