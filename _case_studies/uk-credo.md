# Sections
sections:
  - id: overview
    icon: cpu
    content: |
      **CReDO (Climate Resilience Demonstrator)** was a landmark project under the UK's **National Digital Twin Programme (NDTP)**, led by the Centre for Digital Built Britain and Connected Places Catapult. Running from **2021 to 2023**, it demonstrated how federated digital twins — connecting data from multiple infrastructure operators without centralising it — can reveal hidden interdependencies and cascading failure risks under climate flood scenarios.

      The demonstrator connected asset data from **four sectors**: energy (UK Power Networks), telecoms (BT), water (Anglian Water), and climate/flood modelling (Environment Agency). By overlaying flood projections onto interconnected infrastructure maps, CReDO showed how a flood event affecting one sector (e.g., power substations) could cascade into failures in dependent sectors (e.g., telecoms base stations losing power, water pumping stations going offline).

      CReDO was the first project globally to demonstrate a **system-of-systems digital twin** approach to climate resilience at this scale, providing a replicable blueprint for cross-sector infrastructure adaptation planning.

  - id: timeline
    content: |
      CReDO was initiated in **2021** as part of the UK's National Digital Twin Programme. **Phase 1** (2021-2022) established the data-sharing framework and produced initial cross-sector flood impact visualisations for eastern England. **Phase 2** (2022-2023) expanded the scope, refined the analytical methods, and explored pathways to operational deployment.

      The demonstrator focused on **eastern England**, an area served by Anglian Water and particularly vulnerable to fluvial and coastal flooding. The project concluded in **2023**, with its methodologies and lessons informing the broader National Digital Twin Programme and successor initiatives.

  - id: stakeholders
    content: |
      CReDO was led by **Connected Places Catapult** and the **Centre for Digital Built Britain (CDBB)** at the University of Cambridge. Core data partners included **Anglian Water**, **BT**, **UK Power Networks**, and the **Environment Agency**.

      Additional contributors included **Icebreaker One** (data sharing standards), the **Climate Resilience Hub**, and academic researchers. The project was funded through the **UKRI (UK Research and Innovation)** as part of the National Digital Twin Programme.

  - id: digitalisation
    content: |
      CReDO was inherently a **digitalisation project**. Its core innovation was the creation of a **federated digital twin architecture** — a system-of-systems approach where each infrastructure operator maintained sovereignty over their own data while enabling cross-sector queries through standardised interfaces and secure data-sharing protocols.

      Key digital components included: **flood scenario modelling** using Environment Agency climate projections; **geospatial asset mapping** across energy, telecoms, and water networks; **dependency analysis algorithms** that traced cascading failure pathways; and **visualisation dashboards** that enabled decision-makers to explore "what if" scenarios interactively.

      The project demonstrated compliance with the **Gemini Principles** — the UK's guiding framework for information management in digital twins — and contributed to standards development for cross-organisational data sharing.

  - id: cost-benefit
    content: |
      **Cost:** CReDO was funded as a research and demonstrator project through **UKRI** as part of the National Digital Twin Programme. Exact project costs were not publicly disclosed but were in the range typical of nationally funded research demonstrators.

      **Key Benefits:** CReDO demonstrated that cross-sector digital twins can reveal **cascading failure risks** invisible to any single infrastructure operator. The project identified specific scenarios where flood events would trigger multi-sector outages, enabling more targeted and cost-effective adaptation investments. Its federated architecture provides a **replicable model** for cross-sector resilience planning that respects data sovereignty while enabling systemic risk assessment.

  - id: futures
    content: |
      CReDO's legacy extends beyond the demonstrator itself. Its methodologies, data-sharing frameworks, and federated architecture inform the ongoing **National Digital Twin Programme** and successor initiatives within the UK's infrastructure resilience landscape.

      Key future directions include: **scaling the approach** to cover additional regions and infrastructure sectors; **operationalising** the digital twin for real-time decision support rather than scenario planning alone; **international replication**, with interest from other countries in adapting the CReDO model; and integration with the UK's evolving **climate adaptation policy**, including the National Adaptation Programme.

      The CReDO experience has also contributed to international discussions on **digital twins for resilience**, including through the UN and World Economic Forum, positioning the UK as a leader in this emerging field.
